Shenandoah Sovereign - Brand Identity

Creating a brand system for Virginia hospitality.

Shenandoah Sovereign is a catering and wedding company rooted in Virginia's Blue Ridge region. The identity needed to feel elevated, warm, and landscape-aware without leaning into generic bridal cues or losing flexibility across menus, signage, social graphics, and event collateral.

Result
The final system includes a primary mark, wordmark, monogram variants, and botanical illustrations ready for menus, signage, social use, and branded venue materials.

Extending the brand with botanical support assets

The floral illustrations helped soften the identity and gave it room to expand across invitations, menus, social graphics, and venue materials. They turned the brand from a logo system into a fuller visual world.

Choosing a palette that felt regionally grounded

Color did a lot of emotional work in this identity. The muted terracotta direction brought warmth and elegance without pushing the brand into a louder or more trend-driven place.

Building the monogram and lockup system

The interlocking double-S monogram became the anchor mark because it could hold both formality and personality. Paired with the serif lockup, it gave the brand a reliable structure for menus, signage, and wedding-facing materials.

Exploring multiple directions before refining

Early concepts tested how formal, romantic, and regional the identity should feel. Comparing options side by side made it easier to see which direction felt timeless and which pieces needed more restraint.

Presenting the final identity system

The finished brand had to feel complete and flexible at the same time. The final identity board pulls together the terracotta-led palette, primary seal, wordmark treatments, and floral support elements into one polished presentation.
