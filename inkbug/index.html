The Problem

Most writing tools fall into two extremes.

Writers told me they wanted something in the middle — organized, warm, and creative. Not clinical or chaotic.

Too complex

Scrivener

Powerful, but steep learning curve. Writers feel overwhelmed before they've written a word.

Too generic

Notion

Flexible but blank-canvas paralysis. No writing-specific structure or built-in motivation.

Visually flat

Dabble

Clean but sterile. Nothing about the interface inspires a writer to open it every day.

Design Process

Goals

What I set out to solve

  • Reduce cognitive overload for writers at every stage
  • Create a workspace that feels calm and inspiring, not clinical
  • Make progress visible and motivating
  • Build a UI system that feels intentional and consistent
  • Support structure without boxing writers in
Research & Insights

What writers told me through interviews and surveys

  • Writers want structure, but not complexity
  • Warm, editorial design resonates strongly
  • Progress needs to be visible to sustain momentum
  • Decorative UI should support the experience, not distract
  • Long-form writing requires excellent typography
Information Architecture

Built around the natural flow of writing

The IA is organized into three modes so writers never lose their place in the process. Each section feeds into the next — planning feeds drafting, drafting feeds progress.

Iterations

Two rounds of focused refinement before final build

Iteration 1 → 2
  • Improved contrast across all components

Iteration 2 → 3
  • Added progress rings to dashboard
  • Introduced chapter health indicators

  • Improved long-form typography for readability
