Book Buddy

Designing a writing workspace that supports momentum.

Built from the ground up.

Book Buddy is a writing companion app I created from scratch — concept, user flow, visual identity, and entire interface. My goal was to build a tool that supports authors with structure, clarity, and motivation without overwhelming them.

My Role
Founder & Senior Visual Designer. I owned product strategy, UX architecture, UI design, visual identity, front-end styling, and the component library.

Current State
Beta with one active project slot while multi-project functionality is tested and expanded.

Timeline 6 weeks
Tools Figma · HTML/CSS · GitHub
Focus UX · UI · Visual Identity · Front-End
The Problem

Most writing tools fall into two extremes.

Writers told me they wanted something in the middle — organized, warm, and creative. Not clinical or chaotic.

Too complex

Scrivener

Powerful, but steep learning curve. Writers feel overwhelmed before they've written a word.

Too generic

Notion

Flexible but blank-canvas paralysis. No writing-specific structure or built-in motivation.

Visually flat

Dabble

Clean but sterile. Nothing about the interface inspires a writer to open it every day.

Goals

What I set out to solve

  • Reduce cognitive overload for writers at every stage
  • Create a workspace that feels calm and inspiring, not clinical
  • Make progress visible and motivating
  • Build a UI system that feels intentional and consistent
  • Support structure without boxing writers in
Research & Insights

What writers told me through interviews and surveys

  • Writers want structure, but not complexity
  • Warm, editorial design resonates strongly
  • Progress needs to be visible to sustain momentum
  • Decorative UI should support the experience, not distract
  • Long-form writing requires excellent typography

2 — Wireframes & Concept Exploration

Three directions explored, one clear winner

I explored three visual directions before settling on the final approach. Each direction was prototyped and tested against the research insights.

Minimalist

Clean, monochrome. Felt too stripped for a creative space writers would spend hours in.

Editorial

Serif headings, generous whitespace. Strong foundation but needed warmth to feel inviting.

Editorial Warmth ✦

Soft neutrals, subtle textures, warm accent. Writers immediately recognized it as their ideal workspace.

4 — Iterations

Iteration 2 → 3
